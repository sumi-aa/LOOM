// Mission

Why LOOM exists.

The maritime industry moves 90% of the world's cargo and carries hundreds of millions of passengers annually. Yet the systems protecting human lives aboard vessels have not fundamentally changed in decades.

Alarm bells, fire doors, and lifeboat drills are reactive tools designed for a pre-AI world. Human coordination under panic is slow, inconsistent, and often catastrophic. LOOM changes that equation entirely.

By embedding intelligence directly into the vessel and into every survival capsule, LOOM creates a system that is faster, more accurate, and more reliable than any human team — working autonomously and relentlessly from the first sensor anomaly to the final rescue confirmation.

THE ROLE OF THE GRAPNEL CAPSULE

The GRAPNEL capsule is LOOM's final guarantee. Every other layer of the system works to prevent its use — but when emergency is unavoidable, the GRAPNEL is what ensures survival. Equipped with its own AI, life support, health monitoring, stabilization, and emergency beacon, the GRAPNEL operates entirely independently once deployed.

Unlike conventional lifeboats that depend on crew operation and calm conditions, a GRAPNEL capsule activates itself, seals itself, and begins signaling for rescue the moment it hits the water. The LOOM system has already transmitted the capsule manifest, passenger health data, and drift projection to maritime rescue authorities before the capsule's EPIRB even activates.
